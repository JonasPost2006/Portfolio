C++

Through my high school, I was able to take a C++ course from the Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam. This course taught the same thing as would be taught in the first year of computer science at university. After successfully completing this course, I received an official certificate from the VU.

Fuzzing

My high school collaborated with VUSEC, a cyber security department within the Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam, to provide a course on fuzzing. Fuzzing, in essence, is providing invalid or unexpected inputs to see how the program handles it and if it crashes, to find invulnerabilities. Upon finishing the Fuzzing course with distinction, I recieved an official certificate from VUSEC.

Robot arm

To become more familiar with embedded systems, I made a robot arm. The arm itself is 3D printed, and it can be controlled via an interface on a laptop.

AI learns to drive

As AI became an increasingly important topic, I decided to experiment with AI myself. To do this I made an AI that can drive a 2D car, so that it drives autonomously and doesn't crash into the walls.

Rubiks Cube Solver

As a final project at my high school, a friend and I made a robot that can solve a 3x3 Rubik's Cube on its own. The status of the cube is entered via an interface on a laptop, after which the robot invents the solution itself and then physically solves it. On average, the robot solves a scrambled Rubik's Cube in about 13 seconds. Click here for a video.
